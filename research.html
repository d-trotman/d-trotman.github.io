Research

My research focuses on understanding microbial communities, using tractable experiments, high-throughput genomics, and mathematical models to learn the laws that govern them.

Current Work
2024–Present NYU

Post-Bacc in the Abreu Lab

I am currently using high throughput barcode sequencing, whole-genome sequencing, and RNA-seq to study environmental memory in S. cerevisiae. My research focuses on the epigenetic mechanisms that explain fitness trade-offs in S. cerevisiae mutants, specifically when they are grown in fluctuating environments.

Our findings suggest that different evolutionary strategies contribute more or less environmental fitness trade-offs, depending on their 'environmental distance.'

Lab Website
2023–2024 Matthaei Botanical Gardens

Meet the Mnomen

This project investigated the microbial communities and chemical conditions that supported Mnomen, or wild rice, growth in Michigan. In collaboration with the Bay Mills Indian Community, our collected soil and plant data on Michigan Mnomen beds.

Our research has revealed typical (iso-) chemical conditions in the soil and water that support robust Mnomen beds as well as the unique microbial conditions that benefit Mnomen. We used that data to inform other reintroduction efforts, such as the one we spearheaded at the Matthaei Botanical Gardens.
