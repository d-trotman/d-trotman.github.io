Research

Current Research

My research focuses on understanding biological systems through innovative approaches that bridge traditional disciplines.

Sonification of Plant Response Patterns

Funded by National Science Foundation 2023-Present

This interdisciplinary research project explores how plants respond to environmental stimuli by translating their electrical and chemical signals into audible frequencies. Using advanced biosensors and custom signal processing algorithms, we're developing new ways to monitor plant health and stress responses in real-time.

Our findings suggest that different species of plants produce distinctive "acoustic signatures" when exposed to various stressors, potentially offering new diagnostic tools for agriculture and ecological monitoring.

View Project Details

Mycorrhizal Network Communication Patterns

Collaboration with Forest Ecology Institute 2022-Present

This project investigates the complex communication networks formed by mycorrhizal fungi in forest ecosystems. Using a combination of molecular techniques, network analysis, and field observations, we're mapping how information and resources flow between plants through their fungal connections.

Our research has revealed surprising patterns of resource allocation that suggest forest trees may cooperate in ways previously unrecognized by ecological theory.

Research Facilities

Our laboratory is equipped with state-of-the-art technology for investigating plant communication and ecological interactions. Key equipment includes:

  • High-resolution bioacoustic recording systems
  • Plant electrophysiology measurement apparatus
  • Environmental control chambers for precise experimental conditions
  • Next-generation sequencing capabilities for metagenomic analysis
  • Custom-built sonification workstations for data analysis
  • Field monitoring systems for long-term ecological studies
  • Advanced microscopy suite for cellular and tissue analysis
