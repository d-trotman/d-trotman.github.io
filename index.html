About Me

I'm a biologist working in New York City, with a passion for both science and music. During the day, I study and work with living systems and their fascinating complexities.

Outside the lab, I express myself through singing, composing, and piano. Growing up as the child of jazz musicians, I found my love for music first through the saxophone.

I quickly immersed myself in as many musical styles as I could, building a wide musical foundation in opera and art song, musical theater, choral music, vocal jazz, classical piano, sacred music, and more.

I earned my BMA in Voice Performance from the University of Michigan, where I studied with Stanford Olsen. I continued my classical training, but further developed my love for contemporary vocal music.

Now, I live in NYC, where I compose and arrange for modern vocal groups, write my own songs, and perform.

Contact me if interested in collaboration or a commission.
